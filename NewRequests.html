How to Request Material

Request Rare Books, Graphic Arts, Historic Maps, or Other Printed Material

Catalog

The Catalog is the discovery tool for the Princeton University Library. Requests to view items can be made using the Reading Room Button located on the "Copies in Library" section of individual items.

Request Manuscripts, Public Policy Papers, or University Archives

Finding Aids

Finding aids are the primary resource for requesting manuscript material.*

Requests to view items can be made using the Request tabs located next to box and item records.

*Papyri and Medieval & Renaissance Manuscripts, however, are not indexed in the Finding Aids. Patrons should contact the department directly at rbsc@princeton.edu to enquire about holdings.

Request a Princeton Senior Thesis

To request a senior thesis between the years 1924-2012 Search the Catalog

To request a senior thesis between 2013-Present Search DataSpace

Material Not Cataloged in the Catalog or Finding Aids

Submitting a Manual Request

Books+ and Finding Aids are the primary discovery tools for special collections holdings. Manual requests should only be submitted for material not cataloged in those resources or for unprocessed items. If an off-site item (ReCAP) cataloged in Books+ or Finding Aids is requested using a manual request form, staff will not be notified in order to retrieve the item from our off-site shelving facility on your behalf.
